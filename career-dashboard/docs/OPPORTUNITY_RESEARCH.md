## How the program *types* differ (read this first)

There are three structurally different things people lump together as "apprenticeships":

1. **Earn-and-learn full-time job (best $ + experience).** You are hired as a full-time (or contract) employee from day one, do real work, and complete required learning hours alongside. Example: **IBM**, **JPMorgan ETSE**. Highest pay, most "real job."
2. **Bootcamp-then-OJT (school first, then real work, then convert).** Phase 1 is a full-time paid classroom bootcamp; Phase 2 is ~1 year of paid on-the-job training on a real team; then you convert to a full employee. Example: **Visa** (via Apprenti). Lower pay during training, strong structure, clear conversion path.
3. **Short immersive apprenticeship (16 weeks) → apply for FTE.** Paid contract stint working on real teams; conversion depends on headcount + passing the normal interview loop. Example: **Microsoft Leap**, **Intuit Career Pathways** (learn-and-earn). Great learning, pay is "living wage" not full salary during the program.

## Program deep-dives

### IBM Apprenticeship — best pay + "real job" combo
- **What it is:** A U.S. Department of Labor **registered apprenticeship** (~12 months) under IBM's "New Collar" initiative. You're a paid employee doing real work with required learning hours; graduates earn a **nationally recognized DOL credential** and become eligible to apply for full-time IBM roles. ([ibm.com](https://www.ibm.com/careers/blog/the-ibm-apprenticeship-program-no-degree-no-problem))
- **Eligibility:** HS/GED, **no degree required**, "some knowledge in ONE of Java, **JavaScript**, **PHP**, C#, Python, Ruby…" plus drive to learn. Camron clears this easily. ([careers.ibm.com](https://careers.ibm.com/en_US/careers/JobDetail/Software-Engineer-Apprentice/107883))
- **Pay:** Recent Software Engineer Apprentice postings list **projected ~$81,600–$107,712/yr** (full-time, varies by location/role); third-party estimate ~$27.58/hr. ([careers.ibm.com 116934](https://careers.ibm.com/en_US/careers/JobDetail/Software-Engineer-Apprentice/116934), [Indeed](https://www.indeed.com/cmp/IBM/salaries/Apprentice))
- **How to get in:** Roles post on IBM Careers on a rolling basis; join the IBM Talent Network for alerts. Process: online app → group assessment → interviews.
- **Camron note:** The **PHP** angle overlaps directly with the Compass (Laravel/PHP) packet — a tailored IBM resume is nearly a clone of the Compass one.

### Visa Technology Apprenticeship — the Austin/Texas play
- **What it is:** Two phases via Visa's partner **Apprenti**. **Phase 1:** 15–16 week **full-time classroom bootcamp** (called RTI), employed/paid through Apprenti. **Phase 2:** ~1 year of **paid on-the-job training** on a real Visa team, then conversion to Visa employment. Hybrid (~3 office days/week). ([careers.smartrecruiters.com](https://careers.smartrecruiters.com/Visa/technology-apprenticeship-program), [climbhire.co](https://climbhire.co/career-development/tech-apprenticeships-california/))
- **Eligibility:** 18+, US-authorized, **must be local to the hiring site**, and **must NOT hold/have completed a 4-year technical degree** — the bootcamp is designed to close gaps. Demonstrated interest via projects/self-study counts (Camron's portfolio is ideal). ([climbhire.co](https://climbhire.co/career-development/tech-apprenticeships-california/))
- **Pay:** **~$47,923** base during training → progresses to **~$70,000 (postings) / ~$81k (aggregator)** after successful completion, plus full benefits (medical/dental/vision, 401k, PTO). ([WayUp](https://www.wayup.com/i-Financial-Services-j-Associate-Software-Engineer-Apprentice-Visa-294972570981519/))
- **Locations:** **Austin, TX** (his target!), Atlanta GA, Denver CO, Ashburn VA, Foster City CA, Bellevue WA.
- **Timing:** Recruitment opens ~**January for a May start**, interviews Mar/Apr (non-technical — they assess interest/aptitude).
- **Camron note — the household math works:** Base ~$48k→$70k is below the current ~$100k household, **but** if his wife keeps driving for an Amazon DSP in Austin (~$40–50k), household lands ~$110–120k **in a lower-COL state than CA/WA/NY**, while he gains real Visa SWE experience + a name-brand employer. This is arguably his single best "relocate for experience + hit the Texas goal" option.

### JPMorgan Emerging Talent Software Engineers (ETSE) — highest immediate pay
- **What it is:** A **full-time, 2-year** software engineer position (not a stipend program) for people from **non-traditional backgrounds** with foundational coding skills. You own projects end-to-end with mentorship, program managers, and training courses. ([jpmorganchase.com](https://www.jpmorganchase.com/careers/explore-opportunities/programs/emerging-talent-software-engineers-fulltime))
- **Pay:** ~**$95k–$125k/yr** base depending on location/role. (Highest immediate comp of this list.)
- **Fit:** Higher bar than the others; strong fundamentals + portfolio matter. JPMC has a large **Plano, TX** tech presence — aligns with the Texas goal.

### Intuit Career Pathways — strongest "guaranteed" conversion
- **What it is:** Paid ~7–10 month **learn-and-earn** apprenticeship (partnered with AnitaB.org). Phase 1 learning (e.g., a Full-Stack JavaScript Techdegree via Treehouse, ~3 months, paid, 40 hrs/wk); then apprenticeship phase; then a **full-time Intuit SWE offer on successful completion**. No CS degree or prior tech experience required. ([intuit.com](https://www.intuit.com/careers/programs/career-pathways/), [Aspen Institute PDF](https://www.aspeninstitute.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/New-Pathways-at-Intuit.pdf))
- **Pay:** **MIT living-wage-based hourly** during the program (explicitly *not* a full SWE salary — it's meant to cover living expenses while you study/work), then a real SWE salary once converted. ([anitabindia.org](https://anitabindia.org/apprenticeship/))
- **Fit:** Excellent if the goal is a near-guaranteed conversion into a stable FTE; lower income during the program is the tradeoff.

### Microsoft Leap — great brand, selective, contract pay
- **What it is:** **16-week** immersive: in-classroom learning + hands-on projects on real Microsoft teams (Azure, Xbox, etc.). ~4 cycles/year. ([leap.microsoft.com](https://leap.microsoft.com/en-US/))
- **Pay:** Contract **~$40–48/hr (~$7,500/mo)**, limited benefits, no stock during program. ([getsmartresume.com](https://www.getsmartresume.com/article/microsoft-leap-aspire-guide))
- **Conversion:** Historically ~60–75% to FTE but **headcount-dependent** and requires passing the standard MS interview loop. **Very selective (<5% acceptance).**
- **Fit:** Prefers candidates with some professional background + a technical foundation; worth applying but treat as a stretch/lottery.

### Google Apprenticeship — good fit, but timing gap
- **US SWE track:** 12 months, **80% work / 20% training**, HS/GED, **<1 yr relevant experience, and must NOT hold/pursue a related degree** — Camron qualifies cleanly. ([opportunitiesforyouth.org](https://opportunitiesforyouth.org/2025/10/10/google-apprenticeships-applications-now-open-in-the-united-states-and-worldwide-march-2026-start/))
- **Timing gotcha:** The 2026 cohort's applications closed ~Sept 2025 (March 2026 start). Realistic next window is **~Aug–Sep 2026 for a 2027 start** — set a reminder.

### Anthropic Claude Partner Network — NOT an entry job, but a free credential
- The **Claude Partner Network** ($100M, launched Mar 2026) is for **firms** deploying Claude for enterprises — requires API experience, live deployments, a Claude Certified Architect on staff. **Not** an entry-level program. ([anthropic.com](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-partner-network))
- **The individual play:** **Anthropic Academy courses + Claude Certified Architect prep are free and open to anyone** (anthropic.skilljar.com); only the proctored exam needs partner membership. Given Camron already ships AI-integrated apps, doing the free Academy track is a **cheap, high-signal résumé differentiator**. ([lowcode.agency](https://www.lowcode.agency/blog/how-to-become-claude-certified-architect))

## Education wording — 3 truthful options (pick ONE, reuse everywhere)

Goal: one honest sentence/short block, no implied enrollment or degree, reused identically on resume, LinkedIn, and every application form. **These avoid claiming any degree or enrollment.** If Camron has *any* formal coursework, certificates, or partial college to add, tell the agent and it can be woven in truthfully.

**Option 1 — Skills-forward, education line omitted (most ATS-safe):**
> *Continuously self-educated in software engineering through hands-on project work, building and shipping full-stack, mobile, and systems applications across TypeScript, React Native, Rust, and Python.*
(Placed under a "Professional Development" or "Technical Training" header instead of "Education.")

**Option 2 — Direct self-taught statement:**
> *Self-taught software developer. Built practical software engineering skills through independent study and end-to-end personal projects rather than a formal degree program, with focused learning in web/mobile development, REST APIs, databases, and applied AI.*

**Option 3 — Self-directed + credential-ready (leaves room to grow):**
> *Education: Self-directed software engineering study (ongoing). Focus areas: TypeScript/JavaScript, React & React Native, REST API design, SQL/data persistence, and applied AI. [Add any completed certificates/courses here as they are earned — e.g., Anthropic Academy / Claude certification.]*

**Recommendation:** Use **Option 1** on the resume (keeps the reader on project proof, ATS-clean, no degree ambiguity) and a lightly expanded **Option 2** phrasing on LinkedIn's "About." Lock the exact words once, then never re-improvise them per application.
